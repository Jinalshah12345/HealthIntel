About Us

Are you an older adult with depression? Have you lost interest in activities you enjoy? Are you finding it harder and harder to get through the day? Do you struggle with feelings of loneliness, helplessness and hopelessness? Are you a person worried about the mental health of older adults at your home or surroundings?

If so, you’re not alone!

Health Intel helps you to realize that neither is depression an inevitable part of getting older—nor is it a sign of weakness or a character flaw. We at Health Intel give important opportunities for the detection and management of depression in older adults.

Vision

Depression can happen to any of us as we age, regardless of our background or achievements. And the symptoms of elderly depression can affect every aspect of our life, impacting our energy, appetite, sleep, and interest in work, hobbies, and relationships.

We at Health Intel, believe that the mental health conditions should be treated long before they reach the most critical point in the disease process.

We are committed to address mental health before it gets critical by addressing the symptoms early, identifying the cause and planning an appropriate course of action on a path towards an individual's overall health.

Mission

Health Intel is dedicated in addressing the needs of those with mental illness and promoting the overall mental health of all older adults. Our work is driven by our commitment to promote mental health as a critical part of overall wellness, including prevention services for all; early detection and intervention for those at risk; integrated care, services, and support for those who need it; with recovery as the goal.

How do we work

Health Intel tends to collect data from health insurance companies and sends a set of questionnaires to all the people equal to and above 60 years who are registered with us to understand their mental health based on the same data. The data collected by our web application decides if the person filing it is under any kind of depression. The questionnaire would be sent every four months for any healthy individual and is a case to case basis for individuals requiring support. The people can be given benefits if they fill the questionnaire. Based on the response the individual would be grouped under red, green and amber state and individuals or family members of the individuals or health insurance companies can act accordingly. We identify treatment strategies such as measurement-based stepped care and collaborative care that can substantially improve the effectiveness of treatment in this setting and we highlight opportunities for addressing health disparities in geriatric depression care. We tend to additionally entail the importance of partaking and supporting family caregivers of depressed older adults by organizing various events, providing a 24 * 7 Chatbot service.
